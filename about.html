Meet the Doctor

Dr. Zaccary Pincock

Doctor of Optometry

Dr. Zaccary Pincock is an honors graduate of Pacific University College of Optometry. Throughout his schooling, Dr. Pincock worked at a clinic in Hillsboro, Oregon specializing in neurooptometric exams and testing. He is fluent in Spanish and also worked as an interpreter and a teacher’s assistant in the Spanish for Optometry class teaching others to provide this service to Spanish-speaking individuals. He enjoyed leading a team of fellow students on a volunteer mission trip to perform eye exams on hundreds of natives in Costa Rica who had previously been unable to obtain proper eye care.

During his clinical rotations, Dr. Pincock gained experience in a wide variety of practice modes. At the Community Health Center in Nipomo, California he performed examinations on the underserved population of California, focusing on primary care with an emphasis on ocular disease. He then worked at the Indian Health Services in Fort Hall, Idaho where he specialized in serving those with ocular diseases such as glaucoma and systemic illnesses like diabetes. Dr. Pincock completed an externship focused on pediatric vision therapy and sports vision where he specialized in treating children as young as three months old with large refractive errors and those with strabismus and amblyopia to realign their vision. He also helped patients born with optic nerve hypoplasia, fitting them for specialty glasses so they could drive and work in the community, as well as patients suffering from post-concussive syndrome from traumatic brain injury. Finally, Dr. Pincock finished his clinical rotations at the Veteran’s Affairs hospital in Spokane, Washington where he diagnosed and treated eye conditions such as glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, cancer of the eye, and cataracts.

Dr. Pincock began his optometric career in a rural community in Eastern New Mexico. He enjoyed the challenge of having to treat a variety of eye conditions and diseases. He was also able to refine his skills with specialty contact lenses. From his experience, he has developed a passion for helping others achieve their best possible vision with the aid of specialty contact lenses.

Dr. Pincock grew up in the small farming town of Blackfoot, Idaho. He and his wife have three energetic children—twin daughters and a son. In his free time, he enjoys playing the guitar, singing, football, movies, traveling, paddleboarding, and being outdoors. Dr. Pincock looks forward to serving you and your family.

Dr. Jeanette Pham

July 27, 1975 - February 8, 2025

With profound sadness, we announce the passing of Jeanette Pham, a devoted wife, loving mother, and esteemed optometrist, who left this world at the age of 49.

Beyond her role as a wife and mother, Jeanette was a highly respected optometrist, whose passion for her work was evident in the care she provided to her patients. Her kindness and expertise touched the lives of so many, and she found deep fulfillment in helping others see the world more clearly—both literally and figuratively.
